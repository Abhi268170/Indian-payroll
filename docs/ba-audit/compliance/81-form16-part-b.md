## Purpose
Part B of Form 16 is generated by Zoho Payroll (not TRACES). It contains the detailed salary breakup, statutory deductions, and net taxable income computation for the financial year. Part B is merged with Part A (from TRACES) to produce the complete Form 16 PDF.

## Part B Data Content (as per IT Department Form 16 format)

Part B is essentially a structured salary certificate for the full financial year. It is generated by Zoho from pay run data.

### Section A: Gross Salary
| Line Item | Source in Zoho |
|-----------|----------------|
| Salary as per Section 17(1) | Sum of all taxable earnings across all pay runs in FY |
| Value of perquisites u/s 17(2) | Perquisite value (company car, rent-free accommodation, ESOP, etc.) |
| Profits in lieu of salary u/s 17(3) | Ex-gratia, arrears, etc. |
| **Total Gross Salary** | Sum of above |

### Section B: Allowances Exempt under Section 10
| Allowance | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| HRA exemption | Calculated by engine based on salary, rent paid, city |
| LTA exemption | If LTA component exists and exemption claimed |
| Other exemptions | Per component configuration |

**Note:** For new regime (our v1 scope), most exemptions under Section 10 are not applicable except HRA. Standard Deduction (INR 75,000) replaces itemised deductions.

**New regime slabs (FY2026-27):**
| Income Range | Rate |
|-------------|------|

## Part B Data Source in Zoho
Part B is populated from:
- Pay run salary data (all approved pay runs in the FY)
- Employee IT Declaration (claimed deductions)
- Proof of Investment submissions (verified investment amounts)
- Prior employer YTD data (if mid-year joining)

**Note:** Zoho generates Part B from its own payroll calculation data. The data flows:
- Monthly pay runs → cumulative salary figures
- IT Declaration + POI → deduction claims
- Engine computation → tax liability per month
- Annual aggregate → Part B content

## Manual Override on Part B
The Zoho audit reveals no indication that Part B content can be manually overridden field-by-field. It is system-generated. However, TDS override at the pay-run level (per employee per month) would affect the TDS amounts shown in Part B.

## Accuracy Dependency
Part B accuracy depends entirely on:
1. All pay runs for the FY being completed and not revised post-generation
2. IT Declaration being captured correctly
3. POI being approved (for old regime deductions)
4. Prior employer YTD being entered correctly (for mid-year joiners)

## Statutory Rules Referenced
- Rule 31(3) — Part B to be prepared by employer
- Section 192 — TDS on salary computation
- Section 16 — Standard deduction
- Section 87A — Rebate for taxpayers below threshold
- New regime (Section 115BAC) — Optional until FY2024; default from FY2025 onwards

## Cross-Module Dependencies
- Pay Runs (all months in FY — source of salary data)
- IT Declaration (employee-declared deductions)
- Proof of Investment (employer-verified amounts)
- Prior Employer YTD (for mid-year joiners — "Previous Employment Details" import type)
- Settings > Tax Details (deductor details for Part B header)
- Employee Personal Information (PAN, name, address)

## Key Observations for Our Build
1. **New regime Part B is simpler** — no Chapter VI-A deductions (except employer NPS under 80CCD(2)), no HRA exemption computation. Our v1 scope is straightforward.
2. **Standard Deduction is INR 75,000 from FY2024-25 onwards** — store in statutory config table, not hardcoded.
3. **Rebate u/s 87A** — INR 60,000 for new regime (effective FY2025-26). Must implement correctly for employees near the INR 12L boundary.
4. **Part B generation is after full FY** — all 12 months of pay runs must be completed. Our state machine for Form 16 generation should check this.
5. **Prior employer YTD integration** — EMP003 (Vikram Nair, joined mid-year) scenario requires combining employer YTD from previous employer with Zoho YTD. Part B must show aggregate.
6. **Part B header must show employer TAN** — from Settings > Tax Details.
7. **Surcharge cap for new regime**: 25% (not 37%) — important for very high earners. Store in tax config.
